When The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom launched in 2023, it had the unenviable task of following up on one of the greatest games ever made, Breath of the Wild. With sky-high expectations and a dedicated fanbase eager for more, the game was instantly met with critical and commercial success. But now, in 2025, with time to reflect, does Tears of the Kingdom still stand as a masterpiece, or was it simply overhyped?

The answer is complex. On one hand, the game expanded on Breath of the Wild’s formula in numerous ways, refining its mechanics and delivering an even greater sense of freedom. On the other hand, some critics argue that it didn’t take enough risks to truly surpass its predecessor. So, where does it stand today?

One of Tears of the Kingdom’s greatest strengths is its ambitious world design. The addition of sky islands and the underground Depths transformed Hyrule into a multi-layered playground. While some feared it would be too similar to Breath of the Wild, the extensive modifications, new dungeons, and innovative traversal methods ensured that exploration felt fresh. Even now, players continue to discover hidden secrets and experiment with the game’s robust mechanics.

The Ultrahand and Fuse abilities are arguably the game’s biggest game-changers. Allowing players to construct custom vehicles, weapons, and even makeshift fortresses, these mechanics encouraged creativity in ways few games have before. While some found the physics-based building overwhelming at first, its depth ensured that the more time invested, the greater the rewards. In comparison to Breath of the Wild’s rigid weapon degradation system, Fuse provided a meaningful solution, giving players control over their gear in a way that felt empowering.

Combat in Tears of the Kingdom remains as fluid and dynamic as ever. The addition of Zonai devices and fusion mechanics added a layer of unpredictability to battles, making every encounter feel unique. Whether using rocket-powered shields to launch into the air or crafting explosive spears, the possibilities felt limitless. However, the core enemy variety remained a weak spot, as many of the foes were simply enhanced versions of those from Breath of the Wild.

Visually, Tears of the Kingdom retains the same cel-shaded beauty that made its predecessor a standout. While some expected a major graphical leap, Nintendo instead focused on refining performance and world interactivity.

The game’s vast landscapes, dynamic weather, and breathtaking sky vistas ensure that, even two years later, it remains one of the most visually captivating titles on the Switch.

The story, while more cinematic than Breath of the Wild, still divided fans. Tears of the Kingdom provided deeper lore, expanded character development, and a more prominent role for Zelda. However, the fragmented storytelling approach meant that some players missed key plot beats unless they actively sought them out. While the final confrontation with Ganondorf was undeniably epic, the overall narrative left some wishing for a more structured and emotionally impactful experience.

But was the game overhyped? For some, the familiarity with Breath of the Wild’s mechanics meant that the game didn’t feel as groundbreaking as its predecessor. The open-world structure, though filled with new content, followed a similar template, leading to debates about whether it truly innovated or simply refined an already excellent formula. While Tears of the Kingdom certainly delivered an improved experience, it may not have been the paradigm shift some had hoped for.

That said, its lasting impact is undeniable. The game continues to inspire speedrunners, content creators, and modders who push its mechanics to the limit. Nintendo’s decision to avoid traditional DLC and instead focus on future projects has only increased its legacy, ensuring that the game stands as a self-contained masterpiece rather than an evolving live-service experience.

So, in 2025, is The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom still a masterpiece? For many, the answer is a definitive yes. Its innovation, depth, and sheer freedom set it apart from most open-world games. However, for those expecting a reinvention of the Zelda franchise, it may have felt like a missed opportunity. Ultimately, whether it was overhyped or not depends on expectations—but one thing is certain: it remains one of the greatest achievements in modern gaming.
