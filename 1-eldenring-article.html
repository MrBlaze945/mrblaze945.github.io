Does It Still Hold Up In 2025?

When Elden Ring first graced the gaming world in 2022, it was met with widespread acclaim, revolutionising the open-world action RPG genre. A vast, immersive landscape, challenging yet rewarding combat, and deep lore crafted by FromSoftware, in collaboration with George R.R. Martin, made it an instant classic. But three years later, as the gaming industry continues to evolve, does Elden Ring still hold up in 2025?

The answer is a resounding yes. Despite a wave of new titles attempting to push boundaries, Elden Ring remains a towering achievement. Its intricate level design, non-linear exploration, and sense of discovery still captivate both newcomers and veterans alike. But what exactly makes this game timeless?

One of the key aspects keeping Elden Ring relevant is its unparalleled world-building. The Lands Between remains as enthralling as ever, with its hauntingly beautiful landscapes, hidden dungeons, and cryptic NPCs. Unlike many modern titles that rely on procedural generation, Elden Ring’s meticulously handcrafted world rewards exploration in ways that few games can match. Players still stumble upon hidden bosses, secret quests, and lore-rich areas even years after release.

The combat system is another testament to its longevity. FromSoftware’s signature challenge remains intact, offering precise, weighty combat that rewards patience and strategy. Whether wielding colossal greatswords or casting intricate sorceries, every encounter feels meaningful. With countless builds and playstyles, Elden Ring continues to offer fresh gameplay experiences. The balance updates and patches over the years have fine-tuned the mechanics, keeping PvP duels engaging and cooperative play exciting.

One of the biggest concerns for older games is their visual fidelity, but Elden Ring has aged gracefully. While newer titles boast cutting-edge ray tracing and AI-driven rendering, Elden Ring’s distinct art direction ensures that it still stands out. The eerie castles, misty swamps, and golden fields remain breathtaking.

The game’s artistic brilliance lies in its attention to detail and environmental storytelling, aspects that modern technical advancements cannot replace.

Another factor contributing to its continued success is its thriving community. Modding has breathed new life into the game, introducing quality-of-life improvements, challenge runs, and even entirely new bosses. Meanwhile, the online community continues to share theories, speedrun techniques, and creative builds, keeping the game’s discussion lively. Even in 2025, discovering a new strategy or hidden detail feels like a shared adventure among fans.

But what about its competition? In an era where open-world games are becoming increasingly bloated with unnecessary mechanics and excessive hand-holding, Elden Ring’s approach remains refreshing. Its philosophy of letting players uncover the world organically, without intrusive markers or overwhelming tutorials, ensures that every discovery feels earned. This design philosophy is something that even newer releases struggle to replicate effectively.

Of course, no game is without flaws. Performance issues, particularly on PC, were a concern at launch, and while patches have improved stability, some minor technical hiccups remain. Additionally, some players might find the lack of quest tracking frustrating. However, for many, these quirks add to the game’s charm rather than detract from it.

Ultimately, Elden Ring’s legacy in 2025 remains as strong as ever. It continues to be a masterclass in game design, storytelling, and player freedom. As newer titles attempt to innovate, Elden Ring serves as a benchmark—a reminder of how rewarding and immersive an open-world RPG can be when crafted with passion and vision. Whether you’re returning to the Lands Between for another playthrough or experiencing it for the first time, one thing is certain: Elden Ring still holds up, and it likely will for many years to come.
