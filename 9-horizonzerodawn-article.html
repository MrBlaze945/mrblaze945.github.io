Horizon Zero Dawn captivated players upon its initial release in 2017, offering a breathtaking open world, engaging combat, and a compelling narrative. Now, in 2025, the first official remaster has been revealed, promising enhanced visuals, improved performance, and quality-of-life updates. But how does this remaster compare to the original, and is it worth revisiting Aloy’s journey?

From updated lighting to refined character models, this remaster aims to bring the game closer to modern graphical standards while maintaining the essence of what made the original so beloved. Let’s take a closer look at what has changed.

Visual Enhancements: One of the most noticeable upgrades in the remaster is the improved environmental detail. The lush landscapes of the post-apocalyptic world now feature higher-resolution textures, enhanced foliage density, and improved lighting effects. Sunsets and dynamic weather feel more immersive than ever, thanks to a more refined rendering system.

Character models have also been overhauled, bringing Aloy and the supporting cast closer in quality to Horizon Forbidden West’s level of detail. Facial animations are more expressive, and skin textures now react more realistically to lighting conditions, making every cutscene feel more lifelike.

Performance Improvements: The remaster offers multiple graphical modes, including a 4K resolution mode at 30 FPS and a performance mode targeting 60 FPS. These options allow players to choose between cinematic fidelity or smooth gameplay, catering to different preferences.

Additionally, loading times have been drastically reduced, making fast travel between locations almost instantaneous. This improvement alone makes exploration even more seamless and enjoyable.

Gameplay and Quality-of-Life Updates: While the core gameplay remains intact, the remaster introduces improved enemy AI and combat responsiveness. Machines are now slightly more aggressive in their tactics, making encounters feel more challenging and rewarding.

Aloy’s movement and climbing mechanics have been refined, taking inspiration from Horizon Forbidden West. Climbing ledges now feels more fluid, reducing some of the frustration found in the original traversal system.

For accessibility, the remaster includes customisable UI options, improved subtitles, and enhanced controller customisation, making the game more approachable for a wider audience.

Additionally, a new Photo Mode has been introduced, allowing players to capture the world in even greater detail with additional filters and pose options for Aloy.

Is It Worth Revisiting? For those who never played the original Horizon Zero Dawn, this remaster provides the definitive way to experience Aloy’s first adventure. The visual and performance upgrades bring the game closer to modern standards, ensuring it remains a stunning open-world experience.

For returning players, the value depends on personal preference. While the upgrades are substantial, the core gameplay remains largely unchanged. If you’ve already completed the original game and its Frozen Wilds expansion, the remaster may not offer enough new content to justify another full playthrough.

However, for those looking to revisit Aloy’s journey with enhanced visuals, smoother performance, and refined mechanics, the Horizon Zero Dawn remaster presents a fantastic opportunity to experience the game in its best form yet.

With a stunning visual overhaul and modern quality-of-life improvements, this remaster ensures that Horizon Zero Dawn remains a standout title in the action RPG genre. Whether you're a newcomer or a longtime fan, there has never been a better time to step back into Aloy’s world.
